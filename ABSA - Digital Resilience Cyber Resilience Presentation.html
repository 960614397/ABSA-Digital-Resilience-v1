Financial Services Is the Most Targeted Sector in Africa

South African banks face a disproportionate volume of sophisticated attacks — driven by the value of the data and funds they hold. The question is no longer if ABSA will be attacked. It is whether ABSA will be ready.

The Protection of Personal Information Act carries fines of up to R10 million and criminal liability for responsible parties. Inadequate breach notification timelines alone can trigger enforcement action.

The Cost of Inaction

An Incident Without a Resilience Programme Is a Board-Level Event

The cost of a breach far exceeds the cost of prevention. For a financial institution, the impact cascades across operations, regulation, reputation, and liability.

Operational Shutdown & Revenue Loss

Ransomware can take payment systems, ATM networks, and customer services offline for days or weeks. Every hour of downtime carries direct revenue cost plus recovery, forensics, and remediation — often exceeding R50 million for a major bank.

Regulatory Penalties & Criminal Exposure

POPIA, PCI-DSS, and SARB mandates carry significant financial penalties. Regulators are now actively enforcing — not just issuing guidance. Personal criminal liability for responsible parties under POPIA adds a dimension no board wants to face.

Permanent Brand Damage & Customer Attrition

60% of organisations that suffer a significant breach lose major clients within a year. A public breach event involving payment data or account compromise can trigger significant account closures, competitor switching, and lasting reputational damage with institutional investors.

Third-Party & Supply Chain Liability

ABSA's ecosystem includes hundreds of fintech partners, payment processors, and technology vendors. Attackers increasingly target suppliers to breach their larger clients — and ABSA may bear the legal and reputational liability for a breach that originates in a vendor's environment.

Use Cases — Financial Services

Problems We Solve for Banks Every Day

These are the most common scenarios financial services clients bring to us — and the outcomes we deliver.

Scenario 01 · Ransomware Response
"We Had a Near-Miss. We Can't Be This Exposed Again."
An organisation relying on legacy AV with no 24/7 monitoring experienced a near-miss ransomware event. Leadership demanded a complete rethink of detection and response — fast.
Our Response
Deployed Xcitium ZeroDwell Containment at the endpoint — isolating unknown processes before execution — combined with 24/7 SOC for real-time investigation and automated SOAR response playbooks.
Outcome
Zero successful ransomware executions post-deployment. Mean time to detect reduced from days to under 8 minutes.
Scenario 02 · Third-Party Risk
"We Have No Visibility Into Our Suppliers' Security."
Hundreds of fintech vendors and payment processors with no systematic way to assess their cyber posture — creating significant regulatory exposure and supply chain risk for the institution.
Our Response
Deployed Black Kite TPRI — continuous vendor cyber rating, Ransomware Susceptibility Indexing, and automated alerts when supplier posture deteriorates. No vendor cooperation required.
Outcome
Full vendor risk visibility within 30 days. High-risk suppliers identified and remediation mandated before any incident occurred.
Scenario 03 · Human Risk
"Our People Keep Clicking the Wrong Things."
Repeated phishing incidents despite annual mandatory training. Tick-box compliance wasn't changing behaviour at scale — and the CISO needed measurable proof of improvement.
Our Response
Human Risk Management with psychographic profiling targeting highest-risk users with personalised interventions. Real-world phishing simulations and UEBA monitoring for behavioural anomalies.
Outcome
Phishing click-through rate reduced by 68% in 90 days. High-risk user cohort halved within 6 months. Board-ready evidence of culture improvement delivered.
Scenario 04 · Payment Fraud
"We're Losing Revenue to Fraud We Can't See."
Escalating account takeover fraud, bot-driven inventory abuse, and synthetic identity signups undermining revenue, skewing analytics, and creating chargeback liability — all while traditional fraud tools generated too many false positives.
Our Response
Deployed Innerworks across the digital environment — device fingerprinting, behavioural analytics, and location intelligence to distinguish real users from compromised accounts and bots in real time.
Outcome
Account takeover incidents reduced by 74% within 60 days. Bot traffic dropped by 80%+. Chargeback rate fell significantly — recovering meaningful revenue lost to fraud.
